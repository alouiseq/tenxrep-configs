# Indie Hackers Marketing Blueprint for TenXRep

A comprehensive guide for building presence and acquiring users through Indie Hackers using the "Build in Public" strategy.

---

## Overview

**Goal:** Build credibility and acquire early users by documenting your journey building TenXRep on the Indie Hackers community.

**Why Indie Hackers Works:**
- 23% conversion rate per engaged post (vs Product Hunt's 3%)
- Community expects and welcomes product sharing
- Founders actively looking for tools to use
- Long-term relationship building, not one-off launches
- Lower time investment than Twitter (no daily engagement required)

**Time Investment:** 15-30 minutes daily for engagement + 1-2 hours weekly for milestone posts.

**Expected Results:**
- 1,200+ targeted visitors possible in 6 weeks with consistent engagement
- Higher-quality leads (founders = potential power users + word-of-mouth)
- Credibility that compounds over time

## Platform Overview

### What Is Indie Hackers?

A community platform (indiehackers.com) for solo founders and small teams building profitable online businesses. Members share:
- Revenue milestones and metrics
- Lessons learned and failures
- Technical and marketing challenges
- Product launches and updates

### Key Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Posts** | Main content feed ‚Äî share updates, ask questions, teach |
| **Product Pages** | Dedicated page for your product with updates |
| **Milestones** | Celebratory posts for achievements (first user, $1k MRR, etc.) |
| **Groups** | Topic-specific communities (SaaS, Mobile Apps, etc.) |
| **Profiles** | Your portfolio as an indie hacker |

### The Audience

- Solo founders and bootstrappers
- Developers building side projects
- SaaS and app makers
- Many are fitness-conscious (sitting at computers all day)
- Actively looking for tools to improve their workflow

## Profile & Product Setup

### Profile Optimization

Your profile represents your career as an indie hacker.

**Profile Checklist:**
- [ ] Clear profile photo (face, not logo)
- [ ] Bio explaining what you're building and why
- [ ] Link to Twitter/X account

```
Sharing the journey.
```

```
for gym-goers who want to see which muscles they're actually hitting.
Sharing the journey from idea to launch.
```

## Content Strategy

### The Content Hierarchy (What Performs Best)

| Rank | Content Type | Engagement Level | Example |
|------|--------------|------------------|---------|
| 1 | Success stories with numbers | Highest | "How I got 100 beta users in 2 weeks" |
| 2 | Guides and tutorials | High | "The tech stack I chose for my fitness app (and why)" |
| 3 | Failure stories with lessons | High | "Why my first landing page converted at 0.5%" |
| 4 | Journey updates with metrics | Medium-High | "Week 4: 47 signups, 3 feature requests shipped" |
| 5 | Questions and discussions | Medium | "How do you validate a fitness app idea?" |
| 6 | Neutral build stories | Medium | "How I built 3D muscle visualization in React" |
| 7 | Product announcements | Lowest | "Check out my new app!" ‚ùå |

### The 90/10 Rule

Just like Reddit, follow the 90/10 ratio:
- **90%:** Pure value (insights, lessons, answering questions, helping others)
- **10%:** Subtle product mentions when contextually relevant

### What to Post

**Early Stage (Pre-Revenue):**
```

Here's why: I noticed most gym-goers (myself included) have no idea if they're
hitting all muscle groups evenly. Spreadsheets feel like work, and existing
apps just show numbers.

What I'm trying to solve:
- Visual feedback on muscle coverage
- Identify neglected muscle groups
- Make tracking feel less like data entry

Anyone else frustrated with current workout apps? What's missing for you?
```

**Technical Deep-Dive:**

**Milestone Update:**

## Posting Best Practices

### Timing

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| Best day | Thursday |
| Best time | Morning US/EU time zones |
| Frequency | 1-2 quality posts per week |
| Milestones | Whenever you hit one (don't force it) |

### Formatting

Indie Hackers uses Markdown:

```markdown
## Heading (use for sections)
### Subheading
**Bold text**
*Italic text*

- Bullet point
- Another point

1. Numbered list
2. Second item

> Quote or callout

`code snippet`
```

### Title Formulas That Work

**Questions:**
- "How do you validate [topic]?"
- "What's your approach to [challenge]?"
- "Anyone else struggling with [problem]?"

**Numbers:**
- "How I got [X] users in [Y] time"
- "Week [N]: [Metric] and what I learned"
- "$[Amount] MRR ‚Äî here's the breakdown"

**Lessons:**
- "What I learned building [thing]"
- "[N] mistakes I made with [topic]"
- "The [adjective] truth about [topic]"

### What NOT to Do

1. ‚ùå **Pure announcements:** "Check out my new app!" (lowest engagement)
2. ‚ùå **Vague updates:** "Making progress!" (no specifics = no engagement)
3. ‚ùå **Wall of text:** Keep it scannable with headers and bullets
4. ‚ùå **No value:** Always include a lesson, insight, or question
5. ‚ùå **Ignoring comments:** Reply to everyone ‚Äî builds reputation
6. ‚ùå **Spammy frequency:** Quality over quantity

## Engagement Strategy

### Daily Routine (15-20 minutes)

1. **Browse the feed** (5 min)
   - Upvote interesting posts
   - Save posts to comment on later

2. **Comment thoughtfully** (10 min)
   - Add genuine value to 3-5 posts
   - Share your experience or perspective
   - Ask follow-up questions

3. **Reply to your posts** (5 min)
   - Respond to every comment on your posts
   - Thank people for feedback
   - Continue conversations

### Comment Templates

**Sharing Experience:**
```
This resonates. When I was building [my thing], I ran into the same issue.
What worked for me was [specific solution]. Have you tried [suggestion]?
```

**Adding Perspective:**
```
Interesting approach. I've seen it done differently ‚Äî [alternative method].
The tradeoff is [consideration]. Curious which you'd prefer for [specific case]?
```

**Asking Thoughtful Questions:**
```
Great breakdown. One thing I'm curious about: how did you handle [specific challenge]?
That's been a sticking point for me with TenXRep.
```

### Building Relationships

- Follow founders in fitness/health space
- DM occasionally with genuine compliments or questions
- Offer to test others' products and give feedback
- Cross-promote when it makes sense

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes
1. **Only posting announcements** ‚Äî Lowest engagement type
2. **No specific numbers** ‚Äî Vague updates get ignored
3. **Too long without structure** ‚Äî Use headers and bullets
4. **No lesson or value** ‚Äî Always include a takeaway
5. **Posting too frequently** ‚Äî Quality over quantity

### Engagement Mistakes
1. **Not replying to comments** ‚Äî Kills discussion and reputation
2. **Only promoting, never helping** ‚Äî Community notices
3. **Generic comments** ‚Äî "Great post!" adds nothing
4. **Ignoring others' posts** ‚Äî Engagement is reciprocal

### Strategy Mistakes
1. **Expecting instant results** ‚Äî This takes months
2. **Treating it as a launch platform** ‚Äî It's a community, not Product Hunt
3. **Inconsistent posting** ‚Äî Regular rhythm builds following
4. **Not tracking results** ‚Äî Use UTMs to measure impact
