# Short-Form Video Blueprint (TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels)

A practical guide for creating engaging fitness content that showcases TenXRep's 3D muscle visualization without feeling like an ad. The goal: **educate first, let the app sell itself visually.**

---

## The Core Principle

**You're a fitness creator who happens to use a cool tool — not an app developer pushing a product.**

People follow fitness accounts for value: "I learned something" or "that looked sick." The 3D muscle overlay is inherently shareable because it answers a question every lifter has: *"what muscles does this actually hit?"*

Never make the app the subject. Make the exercise the subject. The app is just the visual.

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. Exercise Breakdowns (Your Current Format)
Film yourself doing an exercise + overlay the 3D muscle activation view.

**What works:**
- Pick exercises people argue about or get wrong (e.g., "Do pistol squats actually hit glutes?")
- Show front AND back view of the 3D model (like your pistol squat video)
- Use exercises that look impressive — pistol squats, muscle-ups, Nordic curls, dragon flags

**Caption style:**
- "Muscles you didn't know pistol squats hit"
- "This is what a pistol squat actually targets"
- "Front vs back — every muscle a pistol squat works"

### 2. "Do You Even Hit That?" (Myth Busting)
Challenge common assumptions about which muscles exercises target.

**Examples:**
- "Lat pulldowns don't hit your lats the way you think"
- "Stop saying bench press is a chest exercise" (show tricep/front delt activation)
- "The exercise everyone does for abs that barely hits abs"

**Why it works:** Controversy = comments = reach. People WILL argue in the comments, which boosts the algorithm.

### 3. "What One Week of [Split] Actually Hits"
Show the 3D volume/balance view after logging a week of a popular split (PPL, bro split, upper/lower).

**Examples:**
- "I tracked a full week of Push Pull Legs — here's what's lagging"
- "Bro split vs PPL: which one leaves more gaps?"
- "The muscles your program is ignoring"

**Why it works:** This is unique to TenXRep. Nobody else can show this visually.

### 4. "Fix Your Imbalance" Series
Show the balance view highlighting an imbalance, then demonstrate the corrective exercise.

**Examples:**
- "Your push muscles are overpowering your pull — here's the fix"
- "Why your shoulders hurt: anterior vs posterior volume"
- "3 exercises to fix the imbalance most lifters have"

### 5. Calisthenics Skill Progressions
Film yourself progressing through a skill tree (e.g., push-up to planche progression).

**Examples:**
- "Level 1 to Level 5: Push-up progression"
- "Can you do all 5 levels?"
- "Most people are stuck at Level 2"

**Why it works:** Gamification hooks + skill content performs well on all platforms.

## Video Formula

### Structure (15-30 seconds)
```
0-2s   HOOK — Bold text or surprising claim on screen
2-5s   Show the exercise (you doing it, real footage)
5-15s  Overlay 3D visualization (front + back rotation)
15-20s Quick insight or takeaway
20-25s Optional: "Follow for more" or subtle app mention
```

### The Hook (First 2 Seconds)
This decides if people watch or scroll. Use:
- **Text on screen** (big, bold, readable): "Muscles you're MISSING on leg day"
- **Pattern interrupt**: Start mid-movement, not with you walking up to a bar
- **Direct challenge**: "You're doing this exercise wrong"

**Never start with:** "Hey guys," a logo, or any intro. Jump straight in.

### Audio Strategy
- **TikTok**: Use trending sounds when they fit. Voiceover works great for breakdowns.
- **YouTube Shorts**: Original voiceover performs better than trending audio. YouTube favors watch time over virality.
- **Instagram Reels**: Either works. Trending audio gets explore page placement.

**Voiceover template:**
> "Everyone thinks [exercise] is a [muscle] exercise. But look at what's actually firing..."
> [cut to 3D overlay]
> "Your [unexpected muscle] is doing most of the work."

## Platform-Specific Tips

### TikTok
- Post 1-2x/day if possible (volume matters here)
- Engage with comments in first 30 min (boosts algorithm)
- Stitch/duet popular fitness videos with your 3D overlay as the "proof"
- TikTok rewards consistency over polish — raw gym footage is fine

**Text on screen (most important):**
- Bold text in the first frame is what stops the scroll — this is your real title
- Priority over everything else on TikTok
- Example: "Muscles you didn't know pistol squats hit"

**Caption:**
- This is your "title" — TikTok has no separate title field
- Keep it short and hooky. TikTok truncates after ~80 characters before "...more"
- Add 3-5 hashtags in the caption. Mix broad (`#gymtok` `#fitness`) with niche (`#muscleactivation` `#pistolsquat`). Don't overload with 15+ tags
- Example: `Muscles you didn't know pistol squats hit #gymtok #fitness #muscleactivation #pistolsquat #legday`

**Sound/audio:**
- Trending sounds boost reach significantly — if one fits, use it
- Otherwise voiceover works well for breakdowns
- Check the "trending" section in TikTok's sound picker before posting

**Description:**
- There is no separate searchable description like YouTube. The caption IS the description.

**Priority order:** Hook text on screen > caption with hashtags > trending sound.

### YouTube Shorts
- Post 3-5x/week
- YouTube Shorts builds subscribers faster — always include a follow CTA
- Add your Shorts to a playlist ("Muscle Activation Series") for binge-watching

**Title (most important):**
- Write it like a search query people would type
- Good: "What a Pistol Squat Actually Targets," "Every Muscle a Pull-Up Hits"
- Bad: "Crazy leg workout!!" or anything vague
- Keep under 70 characters — gets truncated on mobile

**Description:**
- 1-2 short lines that expand on the title. YouTube indexes this for search.
- Add 3-5 hashtags at the end — these act as topic signals on Shorts
- Example:
  ```
  Every muscle activated during a full pistol squat — front and back view.

  #pistolsquat #legday #muscleactivation #calisthenics #fitness
  ```

**Tags:**
- Skip them. YouTube has basically deprecated tags — near-zero impact on Shorts discovery. Hashtags in the description do the job now.

### Instagram Reels
- Post 3-5x/week
- Carousel posts (static images of the 3D model) also perform well here
- Use 5-10 hashtags in caption, mix broad + niche: `#fitnessmotivation` `#muscleactivation` `#calisthenics` `#workouttips`
- Share Reels to your Story for extra reach
- Instagram favors aesthetics — slightly more polished than TikTok
- Collab feature: tag fitness accounts to appear on their feed too

## How to Mention the App (Without Being Salesy)

### DO
- Let people ask "what app is that?" in comments — then reply naturally
- Put "TenXRep" small in the corner or as a subtle watermark on the 3D overlay
- Bio link: "The app in my videos" with a link — no hard sell
- Pin a comment: "App is TenXRep if anyone's curious" (only after people ask)
- Reply to "what app is this?" with: "It's called TenXRep — I built it to see what muscles I'm actually hitting"

### DON'T
- Never say "download my app" or "link in bio" in the video itself
- Never make a video that's ABOUT the app — always about the exercise/muscle
- Don't show the full app UI (home screen, settings, etc.) — only the 3D visualization
- Don't use "ad language" like "features include..." or "available now"
- Don't lead with "I built an app" — lead with the fitness content

### The Bio
Keep it simple across all platforms:
```
Fitness + muscle science
The 3D thing: tenxrep.com
```

That's it. No "founder of" or "download now." Let the content do the work.

## Measuring What Works

### Metrics that matter
- **Watch time %** — Are people watching to the end? If not, fix your hook.
- **Comments** — Controversial or educational content drives comments. More comments = more reach.
- **Shares/Saves** — "Save for later" means your content has value. This is the strongest signal.
- **Follower conversion** — Are viewers following? Check after each post.

### What to double down on
- If a video pops off, make 3-5 variations of the same concept
- Check which exercises get the most engagement — film more of those
- If myth-busting outperforms breakdowns, lean into that

### What to drop
- If a format consistently gets < 200 views after 5 attempts, try something else
- Don't force a content type that isn't working just because you like it

## Cross-Posting Workflow

1. Film and edit in TikTok first (fastest editing tools)
2. Export WITHOUT watermark (save before posting, or use SnapTik to download)
3. Upload natively to YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels — never cross-post with a TikTok watermark (algorithms penalize it)
4. Adjust caption/hashtags per platform
5. Post at different times: TikTok (evening), Shorts (morning), Reels (midday)
