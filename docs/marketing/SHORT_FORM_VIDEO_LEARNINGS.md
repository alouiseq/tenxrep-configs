## Platform-by-Platform Findings

### TikTok

**Audience:** Calisthenics-niche, curiosity-driven, mostly non-followers via the For You Page.

**What works (validated):**
- **"Didn't know muscles" hook on textbook-violating exercises.** Top 5 like rates all use this format on surprising-muscle exercises (frog stand 7.5%, pike push-up 7.9%, wall handstand 7.4%, leg raise→L-sit 7.5%, Day 1 handstand 6.9%).
- **Progression journey content drives follower conversion** (not likes). Day 5 handstand drove +2 followers; Day 1 drove 0. Activation videos get views; journey videos get follows.
- **Wednesday is the strongest day** (691 avg views), narrowly beating Monday (593 avg).

**What doesn't work (ruled out by data):**
- **Unc hooks** ("40 year old attempts X"): 2.7–5.8% like rates, bottom-tier across two data batches. Reserve unc for bio/comments/tone only.
- **Weighted-load variations where the load isn't the visual joke.** Weighted pull-ups: 944 views, 2.4% like rate — algorithm pushed wide, viewers didn't engage. Same pattern with weighted dips would apply.
- **Day [N+1] coming next week`-style CTAs** — irregular filming cadence means the next post might be Day 27, not Day 19. Use goal-anchored CTAs only (`Follow if you want to see if I hit 30s`).

**Strategic frame:** TikTok is your *engagement and follower-growth* platform. View counts plateau, but engagement compounds toward the 100-follower threshold (unlocks audience-activity heatmap and broader algorithmic distribution).

### YouTube

**Audience:** Broader, more mainstream fitness audience than TikTok. Mainstream/isolation exercises (Pistol Squat, lateral raises) often outperform niche calisthenics here.

**The big surprise:** YouTube is NOT a search library. **93% of views come from the Shorts feed** (algorithmic recommendation), only 2% from search. The "search-driven exercise library" thesis was wrong — YouTube is functionally "Shorts feed v2" with a different audience than TikTok.

**What works (validated):**
- **Bare, clean exercise-name titles.** `Press to Handstand` (10.14% CTR), `What a Pistol Squat Actually Targets` (23.65% CTR). Plain noun-phrase titles outperform cluttered ones.
- **Mainstream exercises** that TikTok curation would skip. Pistol Squat, side lateral raise, leaning side lateral raise — all in the top 3 by views.
- **Strong overall CTR**: 4.86% across all sources, above Shorts norm (3–5%).
- **View counts ~2× TikTok per video.**

**What doesn't work:**
- **Unc hooks here too** — the earlier "unc works on YouTube" claim was based on a single data point. Broader data shows `40 Year Old Tuck Push-Up on Parallettes — Muscle Activation` at 1.74% CTR (bottom-tier).
- **Cluttered title suffixes:** `— Muscle Activation`, `on Parallettes` (when not the point). Strip these.

**Strategic frame:** YouTube is your *reach and library* platform. Full exercise library still belongs here (mainstream content gets reach TikTok wouldn't give). But "library" reaches viewers via Shorts feed algorithm, not search.

### Instagram

**Audience:** Mixed (87% non-follower views, 13% follower). Despite reaching 3,284 unique accounts over 90 days, **net 0 new followers**.

**The key finding:** The conversion funnel works *until* the follow step.
- 5,297 views → 266 profile visits (5.0% click-through rate)
- 266 profile visits → 19 bio link taps (7.1% click-to-app rate)
- 0 follows gained

**Interpretation:** People discover content, click through to your profile, sometimes click `tenxrep.com`, but rarely follow. Likely because they go directly to the app (the follow becomes redundant). IG is functioning as a top-of-funnel discovery channel for the app, not as a follower-growth channel.

**What works (validated):**
- **Comparison/vs hooks.** Your one outlier: `Lean push-ups harder than regular push-ups?` — 2.0K views (2× next best), 3 shares, 0.80% engagement rate. The format that works on IG specifically.
- **Family/strength flex.** `Push-ups got heavier (kids on back)` had strong engagement.
- **Standard "didn't know" on textbook-violating exercises** still works (just below comparison hooks).

**What doesn't work on IG:**
- **Progression journey mid-series posts.** Day 5 (168 views) and Day 18 (158 views) bottom of the field. IG viewers consume each post in isolation — they don't follow a series narrative.
- **Conditioning/drill videos.** Press to Handstand drill: 1.3K plays but only 6 likes (0.46% engagement — the floor). Algorithm pushed wide, viewers didn't engage. Same pattern as TikTok weighted pull-ups.

**Strategic frame:** IG is your *app-conversion* platform, not a follower-growth platform. Success metric: bio link taps + profile visits, NOT followers gained. 19 bio link taps in 90 days = the real conversion signal.

## Platform Audience Profiles

**TikTok audience:** Calisthenics-niche, curiosity-driven, attentive to surprising muscle activation. Will follow for the series narrative. Wants the "didn't know" reveal.

**YouTube audience:** Broader fitness audience. More mainstream. Will watch a pistol squat video even though TikTok wouldn't. Less likely to engage but watch time is solid.

**Instagram audience:** Visually-driven scrollers. Want immediate "wow factor" or comparison curiosity. Don't follow series narratives — consume each post in isolation. Will visit profile and click app link more than they'll follow.

**Implication:** "Same content on all three platforms" is structurally suboptimal. TikTok-niche, YouTube-mainstream, IG-comparison-hooks is the curation reality.

- YouTube: ~1,378 avg/video (highest)
- Instagram: ~292 avg/video (lowest)

- Instagram: 2–8% (engagement rate is actually fine; reach is the bottleneck)

### Follower conversion

- TikTok: ~0.2% (22 followers / ~11K views) — below benchmark 1–3%
- YouTube: ~0.1% (32 subs / ~30K views) — same pattern
- Instagram: **~0%** (0 followers / ~5K views) — but compensated by direct app conversion

### App conversion (the real metric)

- Instagram bio link taps: 19 over 90 days (0.36% of views)
- TikTok/YouTube: not directly measurable, but profile visits suggest similar/lower rates

**Funnel interpretation:** View-to-follow conversion is uniformly weak across platforms. View-to-app conversion (when measurable) is the real product metric and is non-zero on IG. **Optimize for app conversion, not follower count.**

## Anti-patterns — don't do these

Confirmed by data, ruled out:

1. **Unc framing in hooks/titles/bios** — bottom-tier across TikTok and YouTube. Keep unc in display name, comments, tone — not on-screen or in titles.
2. **Weighted-load variations where the load isn't visually compelling** — weighted pull-ups, weighted dips. Algorithm pushes wide, viewers don't engage.
3. **Conditioning/drill videos on IG/TikTok** — Press to Handstand drill: 0.46% engagement floor. They serve the journey audience, not the broad feed audience. **YouTube-only.**
4. **Series-specific CTAs that commit to a day number or week** — calendar-day journey + irregular filming = unmet expectations. Use goal-anchored CTAs.
5. **Day-by-day same-format progression posts** — past ~5 in a series, the algorithm reads repeated posts as same-content. Rotate hook variants (attempt count → breakthrough → plateau → then vs now).
6. **Paid view boosts** — for an app, paid ads should target app installs (Apple Search Ads, Meta install campaigns), not vanity view counts.
7. **Treating IG as a TikTok clone** — different audience, different format preference (comparison > didn't know > journey on IG).
8. **Cluttered YouTube titles** — `— Muscle Activation` suffix, `on Parallettes` when not the point, `40 Year Old [X]` — all drag CTR.
9. **Search-driven YouTube strategy** — 2% of YouTube traffic is search. Don't over-optimize titles for search queries; optimize for Shorts feed click-stopping power.
10. **Production polish before content polish** — better cameras / lighting / color grading don't move the needle yet. Hook variety and volume do.

## Ideas to Explore (Action List, May 2026)

Ordered by leverage and ease.

### Start now (high leverage, low effort)

1. **Increase TikTok cadence** from ~2-3/week to 5/week. Hook craft is validated; need more algorithm shots. Recycle existing footage with new hooks if needed.
2. **Pin a saveable tutorial post** on TikTok and IG (e.g., 60-second handstand setup tutorial). Saves are anemic on IG; this addresses it directly.
3. **Update profile photo** to a fitness-coded image (handstand shot, mid-rep on parallettes). The dark coding silhouette is selling the wrong brand.
4. **Bio updates** — TikTok bio locked 2026-05-19, IG bio locked 2026-05-22. Done.

### Test in next 2-4 weeks (medium effort, validated upside)

5. **Add a 5-second face + voice intro** to next 3-5 videos. "Here's what most people get wrong with X." Personality drives parasocial connection, voice-narrated content gets saved more (which IG weights heavily).
6. **Make 2-3 "muscle reveal only" videos** where the 3D overlay is the entire content, not just a corner inset. Examples: 3D model rotates around a static handstand pose, muscles light up one at a time with labels. Lean into the app's unique feature.
7. **Try wildcard hook formats** — one per week:
   - `5 mistakes everyone makes with the [exercise]` (listicle, saveable)
   - `Stop doing X, start doing Y` (contrarian, educational)
   - Reaction to a YouTube clip of bad form with your 3D overlay analysis
8. **Comment substantively on 3-5 bigger creators' videos per week** (FitnessFAQs, Jeremy Ethier, others). Free profile visits when comments land.
9. **Test comparison hooks on TikTok** — the format broke through on IG with Lean Push-Ups but hasn't been tried on TikTok yet:
   - `Archer push-ups harder than regular push-ups?`
   - `Weighted dips harder than regular dips?`

### Bigger bets (1-3 months)

10. **Personal-story content stream** — the 40+ dad building an app while training calisthenics is unusually specific. Monthly progress montages compressing 30 days into 60 seconds. "Behind the scenes of building the app + training myself" content. Builds a moat competitors can't copy.
11. **Stop posting conditioning/drill videos on TikTok and IG.** Keep them YouTube-only. Treat the journey series as TikTok-only.
12. **Apple Search Ads for app installs** — if paid spend ever makes sense, this is where. Target "calisthenics," "handstand," "muscle tracker" search intent.
